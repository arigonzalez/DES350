Rocky Mountain National Park is one of the highest national parks in the U.S. with elevations from 7,860 to 14,259 feet. It also holds Trail Ridge Road, which is the highest paved road in America, with a peak elevation of 12,183 feet. The land also encompasses 350 miles of trails divided into five regions.

Rocky Mountain National Park contains 415 SQUARE MILES of land that protects spectacular mountain environments and wildlife. Rocky Mountain National Park holds a range of activities, from scenic drives that reach up to 14,000 feet high, to wildlife watching and hiking. You’ll never run out of things to do! Bring the family along for camping or picnicking, or if you’re experiencing the park solo, enjoy the peaceful trails or fish in the rivers. The park contains 66 species of mammals, 260 species of birds, five species of amphibians, and one species of reptile. Some of the best places for wildlife viewing are roadside pullouts in open areas of the park. Most commonly sighted animals include elk, birds, moose, lamb and sheep.
