Dieter Rams: Ten Principles for Good Design

1: Good design is innovative

Technology is constantly and rapidly advancing. As a designer, I should stay up to date with new programs and technologies that create new ways of thinking and designing.

2: Good design makes a product useful

Good design shouldn't distract a viewer from the purpose of a product.
My design should be straightforward and should make the user's life easier, not more difficult.

3: Good design in aesthetic

The way a design looks matters. The aesthetic can impact the way a user's daily life and feelings positively and negatively. I feel that designers have an obligation to make the world clear, but beautiful and aesthetically appealing to everyone else.

4: Good design makes a product understandable

Good design is straight to the point and doesn't confuse the viewer.
The design should be precise, thoughtful, and communicate an understandable message.

5: Good design is unobtrusive

Design has no tricks, it is specifically paying attention to the use/purpose.

6: Good design is honest

Good design isn't manipulative, it is honest in its' delivery. My design should display the purpose or use straightforwardly. It should not try
to portray itself as something it's not.

7: Good design is long-lasting

Good design is timeless because it avoids being fashionable. It utilizes no distinct trends, so it cannot become outdated. I hope for my work to be meaningful rather than something that is easily recreated.

8: Good design is thorough down to the last detail

Every last detail must be thought about in design. It is important to be accurate and careful when designing. I must be critical and thoughtful of every last aspect in order to satisfy a range of consumers. Being precise and thoughtful shows the designer has respect for their users.

9: Good design is environmentally-friendly

Design makes the world a beautiful place, however, it should not congest or pollute the visual environment even further. As a designer, I should create a more clear environment.

10: Good design is as little design as possible

I want to focus on the essentials and not overwhelming the audience.
I will try not to overdesign and focus more on simplicity.
